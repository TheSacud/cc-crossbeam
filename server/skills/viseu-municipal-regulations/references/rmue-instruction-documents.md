# RMUE Instruction And Process Documents

Primary source:
- `Aviso n.o 12538/2020`, arts. `17-22`
- PDF: https://files.dre.pt/2s/2020/08/167000000/0033500372.pdf

Use this file for instruction of requests, alteration drawings, heritage-detail requirements, accessibility plan, horizontal-property requests, and `operacoes de destaque`.

## Article 17 - instruction of the request

- RJUE procedures are instructed through a request or communication addressed to the President of the Camara Municipal.
- The file must include the documents and projects required by RJUE, the applicable ordinances, and the municipality `NIPs`.
- Operational consequence for the agent:
  - if the question is about "what do I have to submit", the answer is not complete with RMUE alone;
  - the agent should pair RMUE with the applicable `NIP` and any current instruction ordinance.

## Article 18 - alteration drawings

For projects involving alterations, the minimum graphic set must distinguish:
- existing situation
- intended alteration
- elements to preserve in `black`
- elements to build in `red`
- elements to demolish in `yellow`
- elements to legalize in `blue`
- final proposed situation

Agent use:
- if a correction concerns readability of alteration drawings, this article is a direct municipal basis.

## Article 19 - interventions in built heritage elements

For restoration, rehabilitation, or reconstruction of built-heritage elements referenced in the `PDMV`, including the historic center and the PDM articles cited by RMUE:
- graphic and written elements must clearly define the dimensioning and the interconnection of materials/parts
- minimum detail includes:
  - vertical sections at scale `1:10`
  - construction details at scale `1:10` or smaller where justified
  - details for roofs, dormers, chimneys, skylights, facade trims, frames, balconies, guardrails, and roof-edge treatment

Agent guardrail:
- this is not a generic rule for all projects; it is specifically relevant to protected or referenced heritage contexts.

## Article 20 - accessibility plan

Urban operations subject to an accessibility plan for persons with reduced mobility must include:
- descriptive and justificative note
- drawings at scale `1:100` or larger
- information on accessible routes in exterior space, relation to surrounding buildings, and where applicable interior access
- full dimensions, materials, ramp slopes, guard heights, and stair details in constructive section

The accessibility plan may:
- be integrated in the architecture project, or
- accompany it as a separate annex

Agent use:
- when the file lacks measurable accessibility detail, this article provides a concrete municipal basis for asking for it.

## Article 21 - horizontal property

For constitution of `propriedade horizontal`, RMUE requires a request including:
- identification of the title holder / communicator and permit data
- precise identification of the property
- responsibility declaration by the applicant
- horizontal-property report with:
  - summary description of the building
  - number of autonomous units
  - floor, destination/use, police number if any
  - rooms, balconies/terraces, garages, storage
  - covered and uncovered areas
  - permillage/percentage of each fraction
- identification of common areas and their access
- drawings, in digital format, with fractions and common areas clearly delimited

## Article 22 - `operacoes de destaque`

The request for parcel split (`destaque`) must include:
- title proving legitimacy to carry out the operation
- land-registry certificate with current registrations
- extracts from PDM ordering and constraints plans
- georeferenced topographic survey at `1:1000` or larger
- location plan at `1:10000` or larger
- plan showing the parcel to detach and the remainder parcel
- report framing the request under PDM classes/categories, land-use index, and public-law restrictions
- where there is an existing building, identification of the relevant prior-control procedure and justification of PDM conformity after the split
