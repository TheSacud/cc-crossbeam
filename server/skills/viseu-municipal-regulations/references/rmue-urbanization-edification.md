# RMUE Urbanization And Building Conditions

Primary source:
- `Aviso n.o 12538/2020`, arts. `23-48`
- PDF: https://files.dre.pt/2s/2020/08/167000000/0033500372.pdf

Use this file for municipal design/implantation conditions, annexes, technical integration, and parking.

## Article 23 - general principles

Urban operations in Viseu should contribute to:
- qualification of the intervention area and surrounding urban fabric
- diversified and accessible public space
- coherent relation between new and consolidated urban spaces

Use these as orientation principles, not as a substitute for measurable rules.

## Article 24 - general urbanistic and architectural conditions

During preliminary-information or licensing review for urbanization/building works, municipality may impose conditions relating to:
- implantation polygon form and orientation
- alignments, recesses, and facade setbacks
- saliences over public airspace
- height, depth, stepping of volume, roof shape/slope, soleira elevation, gable treatment, and end conditions

Guardrail:
- These are municipal conditioning powers inside the RMUE frame; do not present them as automatic numeric rules when the article does not provide a number.

## Article 31 - infrastructure networks

- Telecom, energy, and similar networks required by urban operations should be buried, except where technical impossibility is demonstrated.
- Apparent terminals/devices should be coordinated and integrated into the exterior-arrangements project.

## Article 32 - green areas, collective-use spaces, roads, and equipment

- Areas ceded for green space/equipment should have direct road access.
- Ceded areas should form autonomous, clearly identifiable units.
- Green areas should preferably be concentrated and larger rather than fragmented.

## Article 34 - construction

- Basements should not affect groundwater beyond the construction phase; structure should be made watertight.
- Lateral setbacks should preserve equal construction rights for adjacent land and avoid harming openness and dignity of surrounding sets, without prejudice to PDMV.
- Full-width building may be admitted only where planning allows it, and blind gables that cannot later be completed are not acceptable.

## Article 35 - saliences, cantilevers, balconies

- Use of public airspace by saliences/balconies is allowed in the cases and limits described by the article.
- In uncharacterized streets narrower than `9 m`, balcony depth is capped at `0.35 m`.
- The article also allows case-by-case deviation where location, importance, or urban design justify it and no general inconvenience results.

## Article 36 - roofs and attic

- Roof form and materials should integrate harmoniously into the area.
- Attic raising for storage may be allowed if no aesthetic inconvenience exists.
- Reference constraints include:
  - facade-plane attic height up to `1 m`
  - base roof slope up to `25 deg`

## Article 39 - annexes and secondary constructions

- Annexes and secondary constructions cannot harm aesthetics, salubrity, or insolation.
- They must be placed behind the main facade alignment.
- Roof form and materials should respect local typologies.

## Articles 42-46 - floor naming and parking

### Article 42
- Floor naming follows `Decreto Regulamentar n.o 5/2019`.
- `Piso 1` is the floor at public-access level, with tolerance up to `1.25 m` in soleira elevation.

### Article 43
- All new buildings must provide vehicle parking.
- Private and public parking quantities follow `artigo 41.o` of the `PDMV`.
- Collective housing, commerce, services, and industry may require public parking inside the intervention land to integrate into public domain under PDMV sizing.

### Article 44
- Reference dimensions:
  - private garage: `6 m x 3 m`
  - parking place in building interior or open air: `5 m x 2.5 m`

### Article 45
- Municipality may admit justified reductions in the public-parking component under article 43(2)(b).

### Article 46
- Vehicle access to internal parking should be independent from pedestrian access.
- Ramps cannot develop over public space or sidewalks.
- Gate opening/closing should not occur over public space, except in fully justified cases.
