# RMUE Other Procedures

Primary source:
- `Aviso n.o 12538/2020`, arts. `65-79`
- PDF: https://files.dre.pt/2s/2020/08/167000000/0033500372.pdf

Use this file for urgent documents, searches, document return/shipping, petitions, certificates, vistorias, loteamento-publicity fees, `usos mistos`, and `SIR` compatibility.

## Articles 65-71 - general administrative acts and fees

### Article 65 - urgent documents

- If the applicant requests urgency in writing for certificates or other documents, the corresponding fee increases by `100 %`.
- "Urgent" means issued within `3 days` from submission or from the relevant order, depending on whether that formality is needed.

### Article 66 - searches (`buscas`)

- If the applicant does not indicate the year of the original document in a certificate/document request, search fees are charged for each year of research, excluding:
  - the submission year, or
  - the year indicated by the applicant

### Article 67 - return of documents

- Documents attached to processes may be returned when dispensable.
- Copies extracted by the municipal services are charged when delivered.

### Article 68 - sending documents

- Documents may be sent by post if the applicant asks for it, provides an addressed stamped envelope, and pays any due fees.
- Loss during postal delivery is not attributed to municipal services.
- If postal sending involves cash-on-delivery style expenses, they are borne by the applicant.
- Registered mail with acknowledgment requires the relevant postal forms.

### Article 69 - petition for information

- Any petition for information is subject to the applicable fee in the municipal fee regulation.

### Article 70 - passing certificates

- Certificates are subject to the fee set in the urban-operations fee regulation.

### Article 71 - administrative matters

- Administrative acts and operations practiced in the appraisal of urban operations are subject to the municipal fee regulation.

## Articles 72-77 - special fee-triggering situations

### Article 72 - occupation of public space for works

- Occupation of public space by reason of works is subject to the urban-operations fee regulation.
- The occupation period cannot exceed by more than `15 days` the period fixed in the related license or admitted prior communication.
- Even operations exempt from licensing or prior communication still pay this fee if they need occupation of public space.

### Article 73 - vistorias

- RJUE inspections (`vistorias`) are subject to the applicable urban-operations fees.

### Article 74 - `operacoes de destaque`

- Request, reconsideration, and issuance of the `destaque` certificate are fee-bearing acts.

### Article 75 - reception of urbanization works

- Provisional and final reception acts for urbanization works are fee-bearing acts.

### Article 76 - publicity of the `alvara`

- Publication by the municipality of the loteamento license / prior-communication title is subject to fees plus newspaper-publication expenses.
- The loteador must pay the publication expenses within `5 days` from learning the amount, otherwise the effects of the title are suspended.

### Article 77 - endorsements on the title

- Any `averbamento` on the `alvara` is subject to the corresponding urban-operations fee.

## Article 78 - mixed uses

Mixed uses may be authorized within the same building or fraction, without prior alteration of the use regime under RJUE article 62, if all of the following are met:
- real compatibility / affinity between dominant and accessory or complementary use
- the accessory or complementary use occupies no more than `25 %` of the area of the dominant-use building/fraction
- the dominant use is compatible with the applicable municipal/intermunicipal plan and any loteamento regime
- the economic activity complies with all legal installation rules in force at decision time

The applicant must submit:
- legitimacy title
- descriptive note proving the legal criteria
- plans at `1:100` or larger with dimensions, areas, and uses
- declaration acknowledging the legal conditions

Municipality decides within `30 days`, under penalty of tacit approval.

## Article 79 - Sistema de Industria Responsavel (SIR)

Where there is no relevant impact on urban and environmental balance, the municipality may declare industrial use compatible with a prior authorization-of-use title for:
- commerce, services, or storage use in the case of establishments under part `2-B` of annex I to SIR
- housing use in the case of establishments under part `2-A` of annex I to SIR

Compatibility criteria include:
- effluents similar to domestic wastewater
- waste similar to urban solid waste
- noise without nuisance to third parties and in compliance with the General Noise Regulation
- compliance with fire-safety in buildings requirements
- no smoke, smells, or waste affecting salubrity or hindering building improvement

Favorable compatibility is registered by simple endorsement on the existing authorization-of-use title.

## Agent use notes

- Articles 65-77 are mostly fee/process triggers, not technical design rules.
- Article 78 is useful when the case involves partial service/commercial use inside a housing building or fraction.
- Article 79 is niche; use it only when industrial compatibility under SIR is explicitly in play.
