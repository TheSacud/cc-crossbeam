# RMUE Procedures And Prior Control

Primary source:
- `Aviso n.o 12538/2020`, arts. `5-13`
- PDF: https://files.dre.pt/2s/2020/08/167000000/0033500372.pdf

Use this file for questions about procedure choice, process instruction, public discussion, execution period, amendments, and caution.

## Key rules

### Article 5 - licensing, prior communication, authorization of use

- RMUE process instruction follows the municipality `NIPs` published on the municipal portal.
- Urbanistic operations depend on prior control in the forms allowed by RJUE: `licenca`, `comunicacao previa`, or `autorizacao de utilizacao`.
- In loteamento areas, construction/alteration/amplification cannot use `comunicacao previa` if the permit specification does not already fix alignment, setbacks, floors above/below soleira, admissible uses, construction area, and implantation area.

### Article 6 - exemption from prior control is not exemption from rules

- `Obras de escassa relevancia urbanistica` still have to comply with plans, servitudes, restrictions, construction rules, and remain subject to inspection, offences, embargo, and demolition.
- Even when exempt from prior control, the interested party must notify the municipality up to `5 days` before works start.
- For article 25 works, the promoter must keep a `projeto minimo` on site for inspection, including:
  - implantation plan
  - plans/cuts/elevations when applicable
  - title document
  - prior municipal title references for the property
  - work description including area, height, and materials when applicable

### Article 7 - public discussion of loteamento operations

- Public discussion follows RJUE timing rules.
- Loteamento operations are subject to public discussion when they exceed one of these thresholds:
  - `4 ha`
  - `100 fogos`
  - `10 %` increase over the settlement population of the locality, according to the latest census

### Article 8 - suspension for archaeological or architectural finds

- Municipality may suspend licenses or block prior-communication works if architectural elements or archaeological remains are discovered during works.

### Article 10 - building works in loteamento area

- Prior communication for building works in loteamento area cannot occur before provisional acceptance of urbanization works or caution under RJUE article 54.
- Municipality may block execution if key infrastructures are not yet executed and in service:
  - roads allowing vehicle circulation
  - water supply
  - wastewater and rainwater drainage
  - electric power network for the required load

### Article 11 - execution period

- Execution period for urbanization and building works cannot exceed `36 months`, except justified exceptional cases.

### Article 12 - amendments

- Amendment to loteamento license is preceded by public discussion when the loteamento exceeds article 7 thresholds.

### Article 13 - caution

- Certain operations under RJUE articles `23(6)`, `54`, and `81` are subject to caution.
- For RJUE article `23(6)`, caution equals `50 %` of the budgeted cost of the structural work needed for licensing.
- For RJUE article `81`, caution equals `30 %` of the budgeted excavation and peripheral-retention work.
- Authorization of use is conditioned on verifying that no damage to public space resulted from the work.

## Agent use notes

- If the user asks what to submit, pair this file with municipality `NIPs` once collected.
- If the user asks whether an exempt work needs no paperwork, answer carefully: no prior control does not mean no compliance and not even no pre-start communication.
- When discussing timing, distinguish municipality RMUE timing from RJUE deadlines.
