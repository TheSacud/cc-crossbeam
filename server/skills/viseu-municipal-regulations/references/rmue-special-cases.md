# RMUE Special Cases

Primary source:
- `Aviso n.o 12538/2020`, arts. `25-27`
- PDF: https://files.dre.pt/2s/2020/08/167000000/0033500372.pdf

Use this file for low-impact works, certificates of exemption from authorization of use, and impact similar to loteamento.

## Article 25 - obras de escassa relevancia urbanistica

The RMUE gives concrete examples that may be treated as `obras de escassa relevancia urbanistica`, while keeping the RJUE framework.

Examples explicitly listed:
- logradouro arrangements such as garden works and paving, if they do not cut protected trees and do not exceed impermeabilization limits
- tanks up to `1.2 m` high, if not facing public space
- accessibility ramps for disabled access located inside the lot or buildings
- uncovered barbecue structures up to `3 m2`, up to `1.80 m` high excluding chimney, not facing public street
- light agricultural greenhouses outside urban perimeters, removable/light construction, no permanent foundations, max implantation index `50 %`, max height `3 m`, setback `15 m` to public road and `5 m` to adjacent parcels
- pergolas up to `2.50 m` high and occupancy index under `5 %`
- fence with posts and mesh up to `1.80 m`, inside owner land, minimum `4 m` from road axis
- pet/guard/hunting animal shelters up to `4 m2` and height under `1.80 m`
- first isolated construction in rustic land, max implantation `60 m2`, max facade height `3 m`, at least `20 m` from public road and `3 m` from adjacent properties
- annex-type constructions up to `15 m2`, max facade height `2.80 m`, total annexes not above `5 %` of lot/parcel area
- uncovered leisure structures associated with the main building
- HVAC/exhaust/ventilation/heating devices if they are not visible from public space, do not prejudice aesthetics, ensure sound isolation, and collect resulting liquids
- demolition of the low-impact constructions listed in the article

Important qualifiers:
- Archaeologically sensitive areas involving subsoil disturbance still require prior archaeology opinion from municipal services.
- Total impermeabilized area, including main building, pools, annexes, and all low-impact works, cannot exceed `75 %`, unless the surroundings already show a higher proven impermeabilization index.

## Article 26 - certificate of exemption from authorization of use

If the interested party claims the building was erected before the legal trigger date for authorization of use, the request must be formalized in writing.

Evidence examples mentioned by RMUE:
- land registry certificate
- tax record certificate
- contracts relating to the construction
- other relevant documents

The request must also include:
- location plan
- georeferenced topographic survey
- full photographic survey of facades and roof
- optional parish statement attesting construction date

Historical thresholds explicitly referenced:
- before `07-08-1951` for urban-zone buildings
- before `01-07-1967` for rural-zone buildings

## Article 27 - impact relevant or similar to loteamento

RMUE treats certain constructions as generating `impacte urbanistico relevante` or `impacte semelhante a uma operacao de loteamento`.

This matters when:
- a construction in urban land relies on RJUE article 25 due to lack of road/water/sewer/electric infrastructures
- agricultural support buildings exceed thresholds
- constructions overload infrastructure or environmental service levels

## Agent use notes

- Do not classify a work as low-impact from intuition alone. Check article 25 criteria item by item.
- If the work is close to thresholds or has unusual context, return `needs municipal confirmation`.
- Pair article 26 with RJUE and municipal operational guidance when answering legacy-building questions.
