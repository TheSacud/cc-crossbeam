# RMUE Public-Space Occupation During Works

Primary source:
- `Aviso n.o 12538/2020`, arts. `49-64`
- PDF: https://files.dre.pt/2s/2020/08/167000000/0033500372.pdf

Use this file for occupation of public space, worksite protections, tapumes, canopies, trees, and cleanup.

## Core approval logic

### Article 49
- Works that occupy public space with tapumes, scaffolds, materials, equipment, containers, or related installations require prior municipal approval of a plan defining the occupation conditions.

### Article 50
- The occupation plan exists to guarantee user safety and the fencing of work areas.

### Article 51 - instruction of the occupation request

The request must include at least:
- written request to the mayor/president
- liability declaration by qualified technician
- public-space occupation plan prepared by the responsible technician

### Article 52 - licensing process

- Where a license or prior communication implies occupation of public space, processing follows RJUE article 57(2) and the municipality public-space regulation.
- Municipality approves the occupation project within `15 days`.
- Municipality also sets the caution to be provided when collecting the license.

## Site protection rules

### Article 53
- If total sidewalk occupation and partial carriageway occupation are admitted, pedestrian corridors are mandatory.
- Minimum pedestrian-corridor dimensions:
  - width `1.2 m`
  - height `2.2 m`
- Corridors must be illuminated, maintained, and safe.

### Article 54 - tapumes

- For construction, expansion, demolition, or major roof/facade repairs facing public space, tapumes are mandatory.
- Tapumes must be:
  - resistant
  - carefully designed and executed
  - height `2.2 m`
- Heads must carry alternating reflective bands in white and red.
- Fire hydrants must remain fully accessible.

### Article 56 - protective canopy

- In works on buildings with `2 or more` floors above public-space level, a protective canopy is mandatory outside the tapume.
- Canopy height must be above `2.5 m` relative to the sidewalk.
- The canopy must have a border at least `0.15 m` high.

### Article 57 - protection of trees and urban furniture

- Trees, lighting columns, and urban furniture near the work must be protected against damage.
- Municipality may require temporary removal or repositioning at the applicant expense.

### Article 58 - cleaning and reinstatement

- Tapumes, materials, and debris must be removed within `15 days` after conclusion of the works.
- Occupied area must be restored, cleaned, and displaced signage reinstated.

### Article 59 - scaffolds

Scaffolds must meet detailed construction and safety requirements, including:
- firm support points
- solid bracing
- boards sized for at least double the expected load
- minimum platform width `0.9 m`
- guard rails at least `0.9 m`
- fine mesh or suitable screen to prevent debris escape

## Agent use notes

- When a correction mentions public-space occupation, do not answer only with "ask the municipality". The RMUE already provides a meaningful baseline.
- If the case also involves waste containers or debris handling, cross-check the municipality waste regulation.
